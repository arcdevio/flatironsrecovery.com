Coronavirus (COVID-19)

At Flatirons Recovery we have stringent infectious disease screening for any potential client who wishes to admit to our program.

Please see the following screening questions:

  • Have you been in direct contact with any person who has been identified as a confirmed case of COVID-19 or is currently on the CDC list as a Person Under Investigation for COVID-19?
  • Have you experienced any of the following symptoms (unrelated to substance use withdrawals):
  • Upper respiratory distress including dry or productive cough?
  • Sore Throat?
  • Shortness of Breath?
  • Fever?
  • In the past 14 days have you traveled outside of the US to China, Iran, Japan, Europe, South Korea?
  • In the past 14 days have you traveled to an area in the United States currently experiencing outbreaks of COVID-19, especially the states of Arizona, Texas, California, or Florida?
  • If you have answered yes to any of the above questions and/ or are experiencing these symptoms and they arenâ€™t otherwise linked to withdrawals, Flatirons Recovery requires a confirmatory test for COVID 19 prior to entry into treatment.

Additionally, if potential clients are accessing detoxification services prior to admission into Flatirons Recovery, we will work with those facilities to assist clients in accessing COVID-19 testing whether they are symptomatic, or not.

Once admitted into the care of Flatirons Recovery, all new clients will be asked to submit to COVID-19 swab testing at the time of admission, asked to wear a facemask, and will be educated on symptom identification and personal hygiene protocols followed within the facility.

PHP/IOP/OP Office Protocols:

  • Temperature screenings for all staff/clients entering the office facility.
  • Mask mandate for all staff/clients while in office facilities.
  • Enhanced cleaning and sanitation twice daily.
  • No in-person tours, visitors, parent, or alumni support groups.
  • All community recovery meetings via video.
  • Small group sizes (10 or fewer).
  • Only essential administrative and therapeutic personnel permitted at the office space.
  • Some Telehealth services available upon request, and in consultation with treatment team.

Sober Living Home Protocols:

  • Temperature screenings for all staff/clients entering sober living facilities.
  • Enhanced cleaning and sanitation twice daily.
  • No in-person tours, visitors or other support groups.
  • All community recovery meetings via video.
  • All gym time limited to in-house equipment.
  • Small group sizes (10 or fewer).
  • Only essential personnel are permitted in the sober living homes

Tips for Staying Healthy

As with other respiratory illnesses, there are steps that everyone can take daily to reduce the risk of getting sick or infecting others with circulating viruses. You should:

  • Stay home when you are sick.
  • Wash your hands often with soap and water for at least 20 seconds, especially after going to the bathroom; before eating; and after blowing your nose, coughing, or sneezing.
  • If soap and water are not readily available, use hand sanitizer, as provided. Always wash hands with soap and water if hands are visibly dirty.
  • Avoid touching your eyes, nose, and mouth with unwashed hands.
  • Limit close contact, like kissing and sharing cups or utensils, with people who are sick.
  • Clean and disinfect frequently touched objects and surfaces using a regular household cleaning spray or wipes
  • Cover your cough or sneeze with a tissue, then throw the tissue in the trash. If you do not have a tissue, use your sleeve (not your hands).
  • Use a facemask when you are not able to appropriately socially distance yourself.
  • Get a flu shot to prevent influenza if you have not done so this season.
