Individual Therapy

At Flatirons Recovery Center, clients will engage in a weekly, 1-hour individual therapy session in order to develop more insight into the painful habitual patterns that have influenced their substance use disorder, as well as to develop more ability to take appropriate action to begin shifting those.

Topics that may be addressed during individual sessions include:

  • Healing trauma
  • Habitual thought and behavioral patterns
  • Healing and shifting outdated core beliefs
  • Family systems dynamics
  • Recovery aspirations and goals
  • Self-esteem, identity, and confidence
  • Direct communication
  • Relationships
  • Developing healthy boundaries

Group Therapy

At Flatirons Recovery Center, clients will engage in 30-hours of group programming during PHP/Day Treatment programming, and 9-15 hours of group programming during Intensive Outpatient programming.

Family Therapy

At Flatirons Recovery Center, client will engage in a bi-weekly, 1-hour family therapy session, in order to develop more insight into the systemic ways in which substance use disorders may be impacting their family life.

Support Groups for Family Members

Flatirons Recovery Center highly recommends that family members support themselves while their loved one is in treatment. The following in a small sampling of the community supports that may be appropriate and/or available in your community.

  • SMART Recovery Family and Friends
  • NAMI
  • Al-Anon
  • Alateen
  • GRASP
  • Families Anonymous

Modalities

Dialectical Behavioral Therapy (DBT)

Dialectical Behavioral Therapy (DBT) is a type of evidence-based behavioral therapy. DBT is used to treat crisis/ suicide/ addiction and other self-destructive behaviors. It teaches clients the skills to cope with, and change, unhealthy habitual behaviors and thought processes by developing skills in Emotion Regulation, Distress Tolerance, Interpersonal Effectiveness, and Mindfulness.
